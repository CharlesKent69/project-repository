Threats To Orangutans

There are many threats to the remaining wild populations of orangutans. The main threats in today to the survival of orangutans are,

  • Loss of habitat through deforestation
  • Palm oil plantations
  • Illegal hunting
  • Illegal Pet Trade
  • Illegal logging
  • forest and peat fires
  • For the benefit of oil palm plantations

What causes the decline of orangutan numbers?

Quite simply, it's human activity. The boom in palm oil harvesting means their rainforest homes are being cut down and replaced with monoculture palm plantations. Other industries such as mining, logging, and small-scale logging also damage rainforest habitat. As in the United States, when ranchers and wolves have conflicts, farmers often kill orangutans if they get too close to their crops. Unfortunately, this happens when orangutans cannot find the food they need in the forest and invade inhabited areas.
