What are Orangutans?

The Bornean, the Sumatran and the Tapanuli are three types of Orangutans that are in the wild

All the three orangutans are currently endangered, there are about about 104,700 (Bornean), 13,846 (Sumatran), 800 (Tapanuli)

Here are the three species of Orangutans, Enjoy!

Bornean Orangutans

The Bornean orangutan is the third-largest ape after the eastern gorilla and western gorilla, and the largest truly arboreal (or tree-dwelling) extant animal

The Bornean Orangutans have a round face with red-brown hair with facial color ranges from pink to red to black.

Sumatran Orangutans

The Sumatran Orangutans have a coarse, shaggy reddish coat and grasping hands and feet

Sumatran orangutans are thinner and have longer faces than Bornean Orangutans; their hair is longer with a paler red color.

Tupanuli

Dominant male Tapanuli orangutans have prominent moustaches and large flat cheek pads, known as flanges, covered in downy hair.

Tupanuli orangytans have frizzier hair, smaller heads, and flatter and wide faces.
